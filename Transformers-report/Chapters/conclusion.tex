\chapter{Conclusion}

I hope that this report was able to provide you with a foundational understanding of the working of a transformer. We began with self-attention, where we examined how contextual representations are constructed using query, key, and value vectors. We then discussed positional encoding, which restores sequential information lost during parallel processing, and layer normalization, which enables stable training of deep networks. Building upon these concepts, we studied the encoder architecture, masked self-attention, cross-attention, and the complete decoder pipeline during both training and inference.

The transformer architecture used in large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Gemini and Claude is more complicated than this, but relies on these principles. Their effectiveness has led to widespread adoption across natural language processing, computer vision, speech processing, and multimodal AI. As a result, transformers continue to serve as the foundation of many state-of-the-art AI systems and remain a cornerstone of ongoing research in deep learning.
